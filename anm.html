Alexandria National Museum

Originally the Italianate palace of Asaad Basili Pasha, this building was converted by the Supreme Council of Antiquities into a museum in 2003.
Its collection spans from prehistory to the modern era, featuring rare sunken antiquities from Abu Qir Bay, a currency hall, and Islamic weaponry.
It also showcases exquisite jewelry and precious metals belonging to the Muhammad Ali dynasty.

A statue of a priest missing his feet and legs, carrying a canopic jar of the god Osiris, wearing a cloak covering his hands, and with a shaved head.

Date: 1st cent. B.C

Medium: Black granite

Coptic Daily Life Artifacts

A display of Coptic heritage including hanging bronze censers, oil lamps, and fragments of intricately carved limestone and textiles.

Date: 4th - 7th cent. A.D

Medium: Bronze, Limestone, and Linen

Tanagra Figurines

Elegant terracotta statues representing Greek fashion and daily life, often depicting women in draped garments and stylish hats.

Date: 4th - 3rd cent. B.C

Medium: Terracotta (Fired Clay)

Ancient Greek Pottery

A variety of decorated ceramic vessels, including hydria and amphorae, featuring black-figure and geometric patterns typical of Hellenistic artistry.

Date: 6th - 3rd cent. B.C

Medium: Ceramic / Terracotta

Royal Household Silverware

A luxurious collection of silver-plated tea urns, ornate metal tankards, and crystal serving stands from the Muhammad Ali Dynasty.
Date: 19th cent. A.D

Medium: Silver, Gilded Metal, and Crystal

Akhenaten Statue

A masterfully carved limestone bust of King Akhenaten, showcasing the distinctive Amarna artistic style with its elongated features.

Date: 14th cent. B.C (New Kingdom)

Medium: Painted Limestone / Quartzite
