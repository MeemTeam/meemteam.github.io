The Grand Egyptian Museuem

Located on the Giza Plateau, the Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM) is the world’s largest archaeological institution dedicated to a single civilization. Its stunning modern design serves as a gateway to the ancient world, housing over 100,000 treasures—including the legendary, complete collection of King Tutankhamun.

The Gold Mask of Tutankhamun

The world-famous funerary mask of the young Pharaoh, crafted from over 10kg of solid gold and inlaid with lapis lazuli and quartz.

The Khufu Solar Boat

A massive, perfectly preserved cedarwood ship from 2500 BC, intended to carry the Pharaoh through the afterlife.

The Colossus of Ramses II

This 11-meter tall red granite giant is the centerpiece of the museum’s main entrance hall (the Atrium).

The Hanging Obelisk

The world's first hanging obelisk, located at the museum entrance, allowing visitors to see the royal seal of Ramses II from underneath

The Golden Throne

This ornate royal throne features a detailed scene of Tutankhamun and his wife Ankhesenamun under the rays of the Aten

The Anubis Shrine

A striking representation of the jackal god Anubis, who served as the protector of the tomb and the journey to the afterlife.
