National Museum Of Egyptian Civilization

Welcome to the National Museum of Egyptian Civilization, a unique cultural landmark that brings together thousands of years of Egypt’s rich history, ancient treasures, and the remarkable legacy of the Pharaohs, allowing visitors to explore the evolution of Egyptian civilization from prehistoric times to the modern era.

Akhenaten Statue

This statue depicts the Pharaoh Akhenaten, the "heretic king," who is known for shifting Egypt's religion toward monotheism.

Statue of god Nilos

This Roman-era sculpture personifies the River Nile as an elderly man surrounded by children, symbolizing the life-giving floods and the wild spirit of Africa.

The Egyptian Scribe

​Representing one of the most vital professions in ancient Egypt, the seated scribe symbolizes the high status of literacy, administrative wisdom, and the preservation of knowledge.

Statue of Queen Tiye

As the powerful Great Royal Wife of Amenhotep III and grandmother of Tutankhamun, Queen Tiye was a formidable political figure whose influence shaped the golden age of the New Kingdom.

The Royal Mummies

​These royal mummies represent the greatest kings and queens of ancient Egypt, whose legacy continues to inspire the world through their remarkably preserved remains.

Mummy of King Ramesses II

​Known as Ramesses the Great, he was one of ancient Egypt’s most powerful pharaohs, and his remarkably preserved mummy stands as a testament to the mastery of ancient Egyptian mummification.

Coffin of Queen Meritamun

​Known as "The Beloved," Queen Meritamun was the daughter of Ramesses II, and her beautifully decorated coffin reflects the exquisite artistic style of the 19th Dynasty.

Sarcophagus of King Thutmose

Adorned with remnants of its original golden brilliance, this magnificent sarcophagus offers a glimpse into the opulence and grandeur of a bygone era.

Outer Coffin of Queen Ahmose-Nefertari

This monumental coffin once held the remains of the venerated Queen Ahmose-Nefertari, the wife of King Ahmose and a pivotal figure in the early New Kingdom.

The Holy Kaaba Cover Carriage

Historically carried by camels, this ornate Mahmal represents the sacred journey from Egypt to Saudi Arabia, transporting the Kiswah (cover) of the Holy Kaaba in a majestic procession.

Chariot of Thutmose IV

This luxurious gilded chariot, masterfully crafted from wood, stands as a magnificent symbol of King Thutmose IV's immense power and his triumphant military achievements.

Tent of Princess Isetemkheb II

This ancient leather tent, dating back to 1000 BC, was meticulously crafted for sacred purification rituals, reflecting the profound funerary traditions of Ancient Egypt.
