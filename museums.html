Museums of Egypt

The Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM)

Description: Located near the Giza Pyramids, it is the largest archaeological museum in the world dedicated to a single civilization, housing the full Tutankhamun collection.
Address: Alexandria Desert Road, Giza.
Hours: Daily from 09:00 AM to 06:00 PM (Extended hours may apply for special tours).

National Museum of Egyptian Civilization (NMEC)

Description: A modern museum that showcases the entirety of Egyptian history and is world-famous for housing the Royal Mummies.
Address: Al Fustat Street, Old Cairo, Cairo.
Hours: Daily from 09:00 AM to 05:00 PM.
Note: It features an evening session on Fridays from 06:00 PM to 09:00 PM.

The Egyptian Museum in Tahrir

Description: Established in 1902, this iconic building houses an extensive collection of ancient Egyptian antiquities, including masterpieces from the Old Kingdom.
Address: Tahrir Square, Downtown, Cairo.
Hours: Daily from 09:00 AM to 05:00 PM

Luxor Museum

Description: A beautifully curated museum overlooking the Nile, featuring high-quality artifacts found in the Theban temples and necropolis.
Address: Corniche El Nile, Luxor City.
Hours: Open in two shifts:
Morning: from 09:00 AM 01:00 PM.
Evening: from 05:00 PM to 08:00 PM.

National Police Museum

Description: Located within the Citadel, this museum displays historical weapons, uniforms, and documents related to the history of the Egyptian police.
Address: Inside the Citadel of Saladin, Al-Khalifa, Cairo.
Hours: Daily from 09:00 AM to 05:00 PM.

Alexandria National Museum

Description: Housed in a restored Italian-style palace, it tells the story of Alexandria from the Ancient, Coptic, and Islamic eras through to the modern age.
Address: Tariq al-Horreya (Fouad Street), Alexandria.
Hours: Daily from 09:00 AM to 05:00 PM.
